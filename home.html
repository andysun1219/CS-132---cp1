The Akita Breed

The Akita is a large Japanese dog breed. The Akita breed originated from the hunting dogs in the Tohoku region of northern Japan. They were bred to hunt boars, deer, and bears. Akitas were first introduced to America by Hellen Keller in 1938. Akitas are known to be independent and loyal dogs. Ther loyalty is epitomized by the infamous story of Hachiko, an Akita who waited at the train station everyday for his owner years after the owner had died. However, they also tend to be more aloof, aggresive, stubborn, and territorial than other breeds.

Akitas are actually considered to be two seprate breeds: the American Akita and the Japanese Akita. This occured after Akitas in America began diverging from the Akitas in Japan. There are signficant differences between the two types of Akitas which is highlighted in the Japanese vs. American Akita page.
